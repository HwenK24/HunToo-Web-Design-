OVERVIEW          REVIEW

Review

  1. I am glad to give this book 5 stars. To be honest, I DID NOT expect much at all from a Malaysian author writing horror, because let's face it, most local horror writing tends to be deriviative. Long-haired girls crawling up or down basement stairs, toyols, haunted phones and computers, haunted offices, schools, and toilets etc...

  2. The Malaysian horror staples are present in this book (a well and a banana tree in one tale set in a village in Kelantan) but the stories work best when the settings are modern and contemporary. Such as the sinister story, TEAM MACABRE, which reminded me of ...the film "Office Space", but with more black magic. Another story JOE MOTH, is set on a plane and manages the difficult achievement of the double-twist ending, and the story about an unusual nightclub, TRIUMVIRATE, reads like something Neil Gaiman would've written during his SANDMAN days.
    3. ...
  3. Had a lot of fun reading these short horror stories. Nothing too grisly thank goodness (I'm a wimp) though definitely some bone chilling moments with murderous puppets and ghostly aborted fetuses. One of my favorite stories was "Joe Moth," though it was a little hard to read what with MH17 happening so recently.

  4. Thirteen thrilling stories to make your skin crawl by one of Malaysia's most vibrant writers.

  5. Not all stories give the chills. Only some of it succeed to deliver the chills. i love the last chapter. really, really bring out the goosebumps. Anyway, this os better than Horror Stories by you-know-who.
