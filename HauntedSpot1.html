Ghost Hill, Penang 💀💀💀💀💀

The British built a fort on top of this hill. During World War II, a crazy Japanese officer is said to have held public beheadings on this site. He is also said to have terrorized the locals. His ghost is said to haunt this hill, which is now a war museum. But the host of National Geographic Channel's "I Wouldn't Go In There" says that the site is haunted not by ghosts, but history. The museum is a privately owned heritage site where bunkers and weapon nests can be seen to this day. It's like the soldiers forgot to pick everything up when the war ended.
